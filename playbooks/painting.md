# Painting Contractor Playbook

## Buyer intent

Painting buyers are usually trying to solve one of the following problems:

- Improve curb appeal
- Protect a home or building
- Prepare for sale
- Refresh interior spaces
- Repair damage or aging surfaces
- Maintain a commercial property
- Upgrade cabinets or specialty surfaces

## High-value services

- Exterior painting
- Interior painting
- Cabinet painting
- Commercial painting
- Deck staining
- Fence staining
- Drywall repair and painting
- Industrial coatings
- HOA or multi-unit painting

## Trust proof priorities

- Before/after photos
- Project galleries
- Review excerpts
- Paint brands used
- Warranty information
- Prep process explanation
- Cleanup process explanation
- Team photos

## Conversion opportunities

- Request a free painting estimate
- Upload project photos
- Choose interior/exterior/commercial
- Select desired timeline
- Add square footage or room count
- Ask about paint color consultation

## Automation opportunities

- Estimate request routing
- Project photo intake
- Follow-up reminders
- Review requests after job completion
- Seasonal repaint reminders
- Commercial maintenance outreach

## Common weaknesses

- Generic homepage messaging
- Too few project photos
- No cabinet painting page
- Weak commercial page
- No explanation of prep quality
- No clear warranty or satisfaction promise

## Strong positioning angles

- Clean prep, clean finish, clean jobsite
- Painting built to protect the property, not just change the color
- Local crews, professional process, lasting finish
- Better communication before, during, and after the job
