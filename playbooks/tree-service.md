# Tree Service Playbook

## Buyer intent

Tree service buyers often have urgent, safety-driven, or property-protection intent.

Common motivations:

- Dangerous tree removal
- Storm cleanup
- Dead tree removal
- Stump grinding
- Tree trimming
- Property clearance
- Insurance or inspection concerns
- Preventing damage to home, vehicles, utilities, or neighboring property

## High-value services

- Tree removal
- Emergency tree service
- Storm cleanup
- Stump grinding
- Tree trimming
- Lot clearing
- Crane-assisted removal
- Commercial tree service

## Essential service pages

- Tree Removal [City]
- Emergency Tree Service [City]
- Stump Grinding [City]
- Tree Trimming [City]
- Storm Damage Cleanup [City]
- Lot Clearing [City]

## Trust proof priorities

- Insurance statement
- Safety process
- Equipment photos
- Crew photos
- Before/after photos
- Customer reviews mentioning safety and cleanup
- Emergency availability expectations
- Local service area

## Conversion opportunities

- Request tree removal estimate
- Upload tree photos
- Mark urgency level
- Identify hazards near tree
- Provide address for site review
- Call for urgent service

## Automation opportunities

- Photo-based intake
- Urgency routing
- Missed-call response
- Estimate scheduling
- Follow-up after quote
- Review request after cleanup

## Common weaknesses

- No emergency page
- No photo-upload intake
- Weak safety proof
- No insurance statement
- No equipment proof
- No storm cleanup landing page

## Strong positioning angles

- Safe removal. Clean finish. Fast response.
- Local tree removal built around property protection.
- Professional crews for dangerous trees, storm damage, and clean removals.
