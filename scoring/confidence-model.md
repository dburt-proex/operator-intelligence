# Confidence Model

## Purpose

The confidence model prevents overclaiming and makes recommendations more trustworthy.

Every important finding should include a confidence level.

## Confidence levels

### High confidence

Use when the finding is directly supported by strong observable evidence.

Examples:

- Website has no visible phone number above the fold.
- Google Business Profile has fewer reviews than visible competitors.
- Service pages are missing for major offered services.
- Contact form lacks required qualification fields.
- No visible before/after portfolio exists.

### Medium confidence

Use when the pattern is observable but business impact requires validation.

Examples:

- Homepage messaging appears generic compared to competitors.
- CTA wording may be reducing conversion.
- Social posting appears inconsistent.
- Review response cadence appears weak.
- Lead follow-up process appears manual based on visible tools.

### Low confidence

Use when the finding is plausible but requires internal data or client confirmation.

Examples:

- Close rate may be reduced by slow follow-up.
- Lead quality may be limited by broad service targeting.
- SEO traffic may be underperforming.
- Missed calls may be causing lost revenue.

## Unknown status

Use unknown when evidence is insufficient.

Unknown should be used for:

- Analytics data not provided
- CRM data not provided
- Call tracking unavailable
- Ad account unavailable
- Internal sales process not reviewed
- Close rate not disclosed

## Report language

High confidence language:

> The evidence indicates...

Medium confidence language:

> The available evidence suggests...

Low confidence language:

> This may indicate..., but validation is recommended.

Unknown language:

> This area could not be fully evaluated without access to...

## Governance rule

Never present low-confidence findings as certain. Never assign a precise financial impact to a finding without data.
