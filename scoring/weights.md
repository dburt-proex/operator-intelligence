# Scoring Weights

## Default Operator Score weights

| Category | Weight | Rationale |
|---|---:|---|
| Website structure and UX | 10% | The website is the primary owned conversion surface. |
| Messaging and offer clarity | 10% | Buyers must quickly understand relevance and value. |
| Conversion infrastructure | 15% | Lead capture directly affects revenue opportunity. |
| Local SEO | 15% | Search visibility drives high-intent demand. |
| Google Business Profile | 10% | GBP is often the most important local discovery surface. |
| Reputation and trust | 10% | Reviews and proof reduce buyer hesitation. |
| Social presence | 5% | Social supports proof, familiarity, and retargeting but is not always primary. |
| Automation maturity | 10% | Automation reduces lost leads and administrative friction. |
| AI readiness | 5% | AI leverage depends on process maturity and data hygiene. |
| Analytics and reporting | 5% | Measurement enables optimization. |
| Competitive position | 5% | Local performance is relative to available alternatives. |

Total: 100%

## When to adjust weights

Adjust weights only when business model changes materially.

Examples:

- Emergency services: increase lead response and GBP weight.
- High-ticket remodeling: increase trust, portfolio, and nurture weight.
- Commercial B2B: increase authority, case studies, and sales process weight.
- Subscription services: increase retention and lifecycle automation weight.

## Governance note

Any weight change must be documented in the report methodology notes so the score remains auditable.
