# Grading Rubric

## Purpose

This rubric standardizes how assessment categories are scored.

## Universal scoring anchors

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 0 | Missing, broken, or actively harmful. |
| 25 | Present but weak, incomplete, or unreliable. |
| 50 | Functional baseline; acceptable but not strong. |
| 75 | Strong and commercially useful with clear improvement opportunities. |
| 100 | Excellent, differentiated, measurable, and well-integrated. |

## Category scoring process

1. Review evidence.
2. Identify applicable signals.
3. Assign preliminary score.
4. Check score against anchors.
5. Document confidence.
6. Document unknowns.
7. Add notes for client report.

## Scoring rule

Do not punish a business for an unknown internal process unless external evidence strongly indicates a gap.

Example:

- No visible CRM evidence does not automatically mean no CRM exists.
- If client confirms no CRM exists, the automation and reporting scores may be reduced.

## Website structure and UX

0: broken, confusing, or unusable.
25: exists but difficult to use or understand.
50: functional but generic.
75: clear, professional, mobile-friendly, and easy to navigate.
100: excellent journey design with strong proof, service architecture, and conversion flow.

## Messaging and offer clarity

0: unclear what the business does.
25: service category is clear but value is vague.
50: clear services but generic positioning.
75: specific, customer-centered, locally relevant positioning.
100: differentiated message with clear outcomes, proof, and buyer relevance.

## Conversion infrastructure

0: no clear path to inquire.
25: contact exists but is weak or buried.
50: basic phone/form path exists.
75: strong CTAs, mobile action path, and usable intake.
100: optimized inquiry journey with tracking, routing, follow-up, and conversion measurement.

## Local SEO

0: not indexable or no local targeting.
25: minimal local SEO.
50: basic service and location signals.
75: strong service-page architecture and local relevance.
100: dominant local search structure with content depth, technical hygiene, authority, and ongoing optimization.

## Google Business Profile

0: absent or unmanaged.
25: incomplete.
50: complete baseline.
75: optimized profile with strong reviews/photos/services.
100: actively managed, high-performing local discovery asset.

## Reputation and trust

0: no trust proof.
25: weak proof or outdated signals.
50: reviews exist but underused.
75: strong reviews and proof integrated into buyer journey.
100: exceptional trust system with reviews, proof, process, guarantees, and reputation management.

## Automation maturity

0: entirely manual and untracked.
25: basic notifications only.
50: some structured tools.
75: CRM and key follow-ups exist.
100: managed automation layer with routing, reporting, lifecycle follow-up, and governance.
