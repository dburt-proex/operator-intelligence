# Google Business Profile Standard

## Purpose

Evaluate whether the company’s Google Business Profile supports local discovery, trust, and lead conversion.

## Evaluation domains

### 1. Profile completeness

Signals:

- Business name
- Primary category
- Secondary categories
- Phone number
- Website link
- Service area
- Hours
- Services
- Business description
- Photos
- Booking or appointment link when relevant

### 2. Review strength

Signals:

- Review count
- Average rating
- Review recency
- Review velocity
- Review quality
- Owner responses
- Keyword-rich customer language

### 3. Photo strength

Signals:

- Recent photos
- Before/after images
- Team photos
- Job-site photos
- Vehicles/equipment
- Finished work
- Owner-uploaded content, not only customer photos

### 4. Service alignment

Signals:

- Services listed match website services
- High-value services included
- Service descriptions clear
- Categories support search intent

### 5. Activity

Signals:

- Posts
- Updates
- Review responses
- New photos
- Q&A management

## Common findings

- Profile exists but is underused.
- Reviews are strong but not actively harvested.
- Photos do not show enough proof of work.
- Services are incomplete or not aligned with website.
- Owner responses are inconsistent.
- No posting cadence exists.

## Recommended interventions

- Complete all profile fields.
- Add service descriptions.
- Upload project photos weekly.
- Create review request automation.
- Respond to all reviews.
- Add Q&A content.
- Post seasonal offers or project updates.
