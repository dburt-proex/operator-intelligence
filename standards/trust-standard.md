# Trust Evaluation Standard

## Purpose

Evaluate whether the business reduces buyer uncertainty and creates enough confidence for a prospect to take action.

## Trust domains

### 1. Review proof

Signals:

- Review count
- Average rating
- Review recency
- Review quality
- Owner responses
- Reviews mention specific services
- Reviews mention professionalism, timeliness, quality, communication, and cleanup

### 2. Work proof

Signals:

- Project gallery
- Before/after images
- Case studies
- Customer stories
- Job-site photos
- Finished-work photos
- Process photos

### 3. Human proof

Signals:

- Owner or team photos
- Crew photos
- About page
- Local story
- Values
- Hiring or culture content

### 4. Risk reduction

Signals:

- License information
- Insurance statement
- Warranty or guarantee
- Clear process
- Clear expectations
- Safety practices when relevant
- Professional documentation

### 5. Local legitimacy

Signals:

- Local address or service area
- Local project examples
- Community involvement
- Local partnerships
- Local references
- Recognizable regional language

## Common findings

- Reviews exist but are not integrated into the website.
- Website lacks enough project proof.
- No team or owner identity is visible.
- Warranty, insurance, or process details are unclear.
- Trust signals appear too late in the customer journey.

## Recommended interventions

- Add review highlights above the fold.
- Add project proof to every service page.
- Build a project gallery.
- Add team/owner section.
- Add risk-reduction badges and language.
- Add process section explaining what customers can expect.
