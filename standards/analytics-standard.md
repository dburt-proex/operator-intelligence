# Analytics and Reporting Standard

## Purpose

Evaluate whether the business can measure the performance of its growth system.

## Reporting principle

A business cannot consistently improve what it does not observe.

## Evaluation domains

### 1. Website analytics

Signals:

- Analytics installed
- Search Console configured
- Conversion events tracked
- Form submissions tracked
- Phone clicks tracked
- Important pages monitored

### 2. Lead reporting

Signals:

- Leads counted by source
- Service type captured
- Urgency captured
- Location captured
- Lead status tracked
- Estimate outcome tracked

### 3. Sales reporting

Signals:

- Estimate sent count
- Close rate
- Lost reason
- Average job value
- Revenue by service
- Sales cycle length

### 4. Reputation reporting

Signals:

- Review count
- Review velocity
- Average rating
- Review response completion
- Testimonials captured

### 5. Executive dashboard

Signals:

- Owner can see key metrics quickly
- Monthly performance is reviewed
- Trends are visible
- Action items are generated from data

## Common findings

- Website may have analytics but no conversion tracking.
- Leads are handled manually without source attribution.
- Estimate outcomes are not tracked.
- Review growth is not monitored.
- Reporting does not produce clear decisions.

## Recommended interventions

- Install or verify analytics.
- Configure Search Console.
- Track forms and click-to-call events.
- Build CRM fields for lead source and status.
- Create monthly growth dashboard.
- Hold monthly review cadence.
