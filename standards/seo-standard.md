# Local SEO Standard

## Purpose

Evaluate whether the business can be discovered by high-intent local buyers searching for its services.

## Evaluation domains

### 1. Service keyword coverage

Signals:

- Core service pages exist
- High-value service pages exist
- Emergency or urgent-service pages exist when relevant
- Seasonal services covered
- Commercial/residential distinctions covered

### 2. Local relevance

Signals:

- City and service-area references
- Nearby communities included where appropriate
- Local project examples
- Local testimonials
- Location-specific landing pages when justified

### 3. On-page SEO

Signals:

- Unique title tags
- Meta descriptions
- H1/H2 structure
- Internal links
- Image alt text
- FAQ content
- Clear URL structure

### 4. Content depth

Signals:

- Pages answer buyer questions
- Process explained
- Pricing factors explained when possible
- Objections addressed
- Project proof included
- FAQs included

### 5. Technical basics

Signals:

- Indexable pages
- Sitemap
- SSL
- Mobile friendly
- Reasonable loading experience
- No obvious broken pages

### 6. Authority signals

Signals:

- Reviews
- Local citations
- Backlinks
- Press mentions
- Partnerships
- Associations
- Case studies

## Common findings

- Website has general service pages but lacks specific landing pages.
- Local search targeting is broad or inconsistent.
- Page titles are generic.
- Thin content limits ranking potential.
- Blog exists but does not target buyer-intent terms.
- Competitors have stronger local service coverage.

## Recommended interventions

- Build service-specific landing pages.
- Build location-specific pages selectively.
- Add project case studies.
- Add FAQ schema where appropriate.
- Improve internal linking.
- Rewrite title tags and meta descriptions.
- Create seasonal service content.
- Build citation and review strategy.
