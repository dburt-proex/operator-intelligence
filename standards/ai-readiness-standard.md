# AI Readiness Standard

## Purpose

Evaluate whether the business has enough process clarity, data hygiene, and operational control to benefit from AI systems safely and effectively.

## AI readiness principle

AI should not be added to a chaotic workflow. AI amplifies the quality of the system it is placed inside.

## Evaluation domains

### 1. Process clarity

Signals:

- Lead process is documented
- Estimate process is documented
- Customer communication standards exist
- Handoff points are clear
- Escalation rules exist

### 2. Data hygiene

Signals:

- Leads are stored consistently
- Customer records are structured
- Service categories are clear
- Status fields exist
- Historical outcomes are trackable

### 3. Risk controls

Signals:

- AI tools do not make binding promises without review
- Sensitive data is protected
- Human review gates exist
- Customer-facing AI has boundaries
- Escalation rules are defined

### 4. Use-case fit

Good AI use cases:

- FAQ assistant
- Intake assistant
- Estimate preparation support
- Review response drafting
- Follow-up drafting
- Content drafting
- Lead qualification
- Internal knowledge assistant

Poor early AI use cases:

- Fully automated pricing without controls
- Binding commitments without review
- Unsupervised customer escalation handling
- Replacing operational process design

## Readiness tiers

| Tier | Description |
|---:|---|
| 0 | No process structure. AI would increase confusion. |
| 1 | Basic processes exist, but data is inconsistent. |
| 2 | Some structured workflows exist; limited AI pilots possible. |
| 3 | Data and workflows are organized enough for controlled AI assistance. |
| 4 | AI can support multiple workflows with human review. |
| 5 | Business has governed AI workflows with audit trails and continuous improvement. |

## Recommended interventions

- Document lead and estimate workflows.
- Standardize customer intake fields.
- Add CRM structure.
- Define AI usage policy.
- Add human review gates.
- Start with low-risk assistant workflows.
- Add audit logs for AI-generated customer communication.
