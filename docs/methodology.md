# Assessment Methodology

## Purpose

The Business Growth Systems Assessment evaluates how effectively a company’s digital and operational infrastructure turns market demand into revenue.

The methodology treats the business as a connected system rather than a set of isolated assets.

## Assessment lifecycle

1. **Client intake**
   - Business type
   - Services offered
   - Service area
   - Revenue goals
   - Current lead sources
   - Current pain points
   - Existing tools

2. **Surface mapping**
   - Website
   - Google Business Profile
   - Facebook page
   - Other social profiles
   - Review platforms
   - Local directories
   - Ads, if visible
   - Booking/contact channels

3. **Evidence capture**
   - Screenshots
   - URLs
   - Search observations
   - Review patterns
   - CTA inventory
   - Competitor examples
   - Client interview notes

4. **Signal scoring**
   - Quality
   - Relevance
   - Confidence
   - Actionability
   - Business impact

5. **Category scoring**
   - Website performance
   - UX and customer journey
   - Messaging and offer clarity
   - Local SEO
   - Google Business Profile
   - Reputation and trust
   - Social presence
   - Conversion infrastructure
   - Automation readiness
   - AI readiness
   - Analytics and reporting
   - Competitive position

6. **Operator Score calculation**
   - Apply category weights
   - Normalize unknowns
   - Document assumptions
   - Assign maturity tier

7. **Finding synthesis**
   - Identify strengths
   - Identify constraints
   - Identify revenue leakage
   - Identify quick wins
   - Identify structural fixes
   - Identify growth investments

8. **Recommendation routing**
   - Map finding to business impact
   - Score implementation effort
   - Score urgency
   - Score confidence
   - Assign priority

9. **Roadmap generation**
   - Phase 1: quick wins
   - Phase 2: growth foundation
   - Phase 3: automation and scale
   - Phase 4: optimization and monitoring

10. **Executive delivery**
   - Present scorecard
   - Explain highest-impact findings
   - Review roadmap
   - Recommend implementation package

## Finding quality standard

Every finding should include:

- Observation
- Evidence
- Interpretation
- Business impact
- Confidence
- Priority
- Recommendation
- Implementation path

## Recommendation format

Each recommendation should answer:

1. What should change?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What evidence supports it?
4. How difficult is implementation?
5. What result should improve?
6. What is the next action?

## Scope boundaries

The assessment may infer likely business impact from observable evidence, but it must not claim exact revenue, traffic, conversion, or ranking outcomes unless the client provides data.

## Governance rule

A recommendation cannot enter the final report until it passes the following gates:

- Evidence exists
- Business impact is defined
- Confidence is assigned
- Priority is justified
- Implementation path is realistic
