# Operating Principles

## 1. Growth is a system

A website is not evaluated as a standalone asset. It is evaluated as part of a larger customer acquisition and operational execution system.

## 2. Evidence before opinion

No finding should be presented as truth unless it is supported by observable evidence.

Evidence may include:

- Website content
- Page structure
- Calls to action
- Search visibility
- Google Business Profile data
- Review profile
- Social activity
- Competitor comparison
- Lead handling process
- CRM workflow
- Analytics data
- Interview responses

## 3. Every finding must map to business impact

Aesthetic preferences are not enough. Each finding must connect to one or more business outcomes:

- Lead volume
- Lead quality
- Conversion rate
- Customer trust
- Speed to contact
- Estimate booking
- Close rate
- Customer retention
- Referral generation
- Operational efficiency
- Competitive position

## 4. Score only what can be reasonably observed

If evidence is unavailable, mark the item as unknown instead of guessing.

Unknown is not the same as bad. Unknown means more discovery is needed.

## 5. Confidence matters

Every recommendation should include confidence level:

- High confidence: directly supported by strong evidence
- Medium confidence: supported by observable pattern but requires validation
- Low confidence: plausible but requires client input or analytics access

## 6. Prioritize leverage, not volume

A report with 100 low-priority recommendations is weaker than a report with 10 properly sequenced high-leverage actions.

## 7. Separate diagnosis from implementation

The assessment identifies what is happening and why it matters. The implementation roadmap defines what to do about it.

## 8. Build for repeatability

Every process should be teachable, repeatable, and auditable.

## 9. Use governance gates

Recommendations move through gates:

1. Intake
2. Evidence capture
3. Signal scoring
4. Confidence assignment
5. Impact mapping
6. Priority ranking
7. Recommendation drafting
8. Executive review
9. Client delivery

## 10. Never overstate ROI

Revenue projections must be framed as assumptions, scenarios, or modeled estimates unless directly supported by client data.
