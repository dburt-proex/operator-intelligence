# Terminology

This document defines the internal language of Operator Intelligence.

## Operator Intelligence™

The umbrella methodology for assessing and improving the business growth operating system of a company.

## Business Growth Systems Assessment

The structured assessment process used to evaluate digital presence, customer acquisition, lead conversion, operational workflow, automation readiness, AI readiness, and competitive position.

## Operator Score™

The overall weighted maturity score assigned to a company after assessment.

The Operator Score represents how effectively the company’s growth infrastructure converts market attention into qualified opportunities, booked jobs, retained customers, and measurable operational learning.

## Revenue Leakage Index™

A score representing the severity of missed growth opportunities across the customer journey.

Leakage may occur through:

- Poor search visibility
- Weak calls to action
- Slow lead response
- Weak trust signals
- No follow-up system
- No review generation
- No CRM
- Poor offer clarity
- Untracked estimate pipeline

## Automation Quotient™

A score representing how much repetitive operational work is supported by systems instead of manual memory.

Signals include:

- Lead capture automation
- Estimate scheduling
- Missed-call text back
- CRM use
- Follow-up sequences
- Review requests
- Reporting dashboards
- Internal task routing

## AI Readiness Index™

A score representing whether the business has enough structure, data hygiene, process clarity, and operational discipline to benefit from AI tools safely and effectively.

AI readiness is not measured by whether the business currently uses AI. It is measured by whether AI can be introduced without creating chaos, hallucinated commitments, privacy risk, or unreliable execution.

## Competitive Pressure Score™

A score representing the intensity of local competitive threat based on competitor visibility, reviews, messaging, offer strength, service pages, and conversion infrastructure.

## Trust Index

A measure of how strongly the business reduces buyer hesitation.

Signals include:

- Reviews
- Testimonials
- Before/after proof
- Team photos
- Licenses
- Insurance
- Guarantees
- Case studies
- Project galleries
- Local reputation
- Clear contact information

## Signal Density

The amount of useful, trust-building, conversion-supporting evidence visible across the company’s digital presence.

High signal density means buyers can quickly understand what the company does, why it is credible, where it serves, what results it produces, and how to take the next step.

## Evidence Confidence

The confidence level assigned to a finding based on evidence quality.

## Growth Constraint

A structural weakness that limits the company’s ability to generate, convert, fulfill, or retain business.

## Quick Win

A recommendation with low implementation effort and meaningful expected impact.

## Structural Fix

A recommendation that requires deeper implementation but improves the operating capacity of the business.

## Growth Investment

A strategic initiative that requires more time, cost, or operational commitment but may create durable competitive advantage.

## Decision Ledger

A structured record of the evidence, interpretation, recommendation, confidence, impact, effort, and priority behind each recommendation.
