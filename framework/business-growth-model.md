# Business Growth Model

Operator Intelligence models business growth as a connected operating system.

## Core model

```text
Market Attention
↓
Visibility
↓
Trust
↓
Offer Clarity
↓
Conversion
↓
Lead Handling
↓
Estimate Process
↓
Follow-Up
↓
Sale
↓
Fulfillment
↓
Review
↓
Referral
↓
Repeat Demand
```

A weakness at any stage can reduce total business performance.

## Growth infrastructure components

### 1. Visibility

Can buyers find the company when they need the service?

Signals:

- Local search rankings
- Google Business Profile visibility
- Service pages
- Local citations
- Social discoverability
- Referral presence

### 2. Trust

Does the business reduce buyer hesitation quickly?

Signals:

- Reviews
- Project proof
- Team identity
- Licenses and insurance
- Warranty or guarantee
- Professional presentation
- Clear contact information

### 3. Offer clarity

Does the buyer understand what the company does and why it is relevant?

Signals:

- Clear services
- Clear service area
- Clear outcomes
- Clear differentiators
- Strong calls to action

### 4. Conversion

Can interested buyers take action easily?

Signals:

- Phone number visibility
- Estimate forms
- Mobile usability
- Scheduling options
- Photo upload
- Response expectations
- Low-friction CTAs

### 5. Lead handling

Are leads captured, routed, and followed up consistently?

Signals:

- CRM
- Lead owner
- Response time
- Missed-call recovery
- Follow-up sequence
- Estimate tracking

### 6. Retention and reputation

Does every completed job create future value?

Signals:

- Review requests
- Referral prompts
- Maintenance plans
- Customer reactivation
- Seasonal campaigns
- Case studies

## Revenue leakage

Revenue leakage occurs when demand exists but is not captured, converted, followed up, or retained.

Examples:

- A buyer finds the website but does not trust it.
- A buyer wants an estimate but the form is too weak.
- A buyer calls after hours and no one follows up.
- A lead receives an estimate but no follow-up.
- A happy customer never gets asked for a review.
- A past customer is never reactivated.

## Assessment goal

The goal is to identify which part of the system creates the greatest constraint and then recommend the highest-leverage intervention.
