# Operator Score™

## Definition

The Operator Score is the weighted maturity score that represents how effectively a business turns market attention into qualified opportunities, booked work, retained customers, and operational learning.

It is not a website score. It is a business growth infrastructure score.

## Formula

Operator Score = sum(category score × category weight)

Unknown or unavailable categories should be documented. If a category cannot be scored, use one of two approaches:

1. Exclude the category and normalize remaining weights.
2. Assign an unknown status and show score range instead of exact score.

## Recommended category weights

| Category | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Website structure and UX | 10% |
| Messaging and offer clarity | 10% |
| Conversion infrastructure | 15% |
| Local SEO | 15% |
| Google Business Profile | 10% |
| Reputation and trust | 10% |
| Social presence | 5% |
| Automation maturity | 10% |
| AI readiness | 5% |
| Analytics and reporting | 5% |
| Competitive position | 5% |

Total: 100%

## Score bands

| Score | Tier | Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 0–39 | Foundational Risk | Growth infrastructure is weak, disconnected, or mostly absent. |
| 40–59 | Developing | Some useful assets exist, but the system leaks opportunity. |
| 60–74 | Growth Ready | Core assets exist, but optimization and automation are needed. |
| 75–89 | High Performing | Strong growth system with clear opportunities for refinement. |
| 90–100 | Market Leader | Durable competitive advantage and mature operating discipline. |

## Interpretation rules

A high Operator Score means the company is structurally positioned to acquire, convert, and retain customers more predictably.

A low Operator Score does not mean the company is bad. It means growth currently depends too heavily on manual effort, referrals, owner involvement, or unmeasured channels.

## Use in client reports

Always explain the score in plain business language.

Bad:

> Your UX score is 61.

Better:

> Your website has the required structure, but the current customer journey asks visitors to work too hard before requesting an estimate. This creates a conversion constraint.

## Quality control

The Operator Score should never be used as a gimmick. It must be supported by documented scoring criteria and evidence.
