# Operator Intelligence Brand Rules

Version: v1.0 commercial asset specification  
Stage alignment: Stage 9 — `assets/`  
Folder alignment: `assets/`  
Status: Canonical brand-use rules

## 1. Purpose

Define how the Operator Intelligence name, visual identity, voice, marks, and commercial materials are used consistently across reports, proposals, demonstrations, sales materials, and implementation artifacts.

Brand expression cannot override evidence, publication, confidentiality, or authorization controls.

## 2. Brand position

Operator Intelligence is a governed Business Growth Systems Assessment for contractor and local-service businesses. It converts observable evidence into scores, findings, recommendations, package eligibility, roadmaps, reports, and controlled implementation decisions.

It is not positioned as:

- a generic website audit
- a guaranteed growth or ROI service
- an autonomous business authority
- a package catalog disguised as diagnosis
- a ranking, lead, revenue, or conversion guarantee

## 3. Naming

Preferred:

- `Operator Intelligence`
- `Operator Intelligence™` in designated commercial title use
- `Business Growth Systems Assessment`
- `Operator Score`

Avoid:

- shortening the product to ambiguous initials in client-facing titles
- renaming canonical packages
- presenting CASA, VIL, PromptBP, or DecisionLedger as client-facing product features unless explanation is useful
- creating new branded terminology where existing controlled vocabulary is sufficient

## 4. Voice

Use:

- direct, executive-safe language
- evidence-aware conclusions
- bounded business impact
- explicit uncertainty and next gates
- practical implementation paths

Avoid:

- blame
- hype
- fear-based revenue leakage claims
- guaranteed transformation language
- broad AI claims
- unsupported competitive superiority
- technical jargon that does not improve the client decision

## 5. Visual identity

Core visual tone:

- dark ink
- silver structure
- muted gold brand accent
- white/paper space
- blueprint/system-map accent
- restrained ALLOW/REVIEW/HALT semantic colors

Rules:

- gold indicates brand emphasis, not success or high score
- do not use gradients that reduce data legibility
- avoid stock-photo-heavy layouts
- use project/client imagery only with permission and evidence context
- use simple geometric marks and systems diagrams
- no decorative “AI brain,” robot, or glowing circuit imagery as default brand language

## 6. Mark and lockup

Approved lockup components:

```text
Operator Intelligence
Business Growth Systems Assessment
```

Rules:

- preserve clear space around the mark
- do not distort, rotate, outline, or apply effects that reduce legibility
- do not place the mark over evidence tables or score visuals
- publication state and synthetic/confidential labels must remain at least as visible as promotional marks where required

## 7. Commercial claims

Approved:

- governed, evidence-backed assessment
- repeatable scoring and decision records
- controlled recommendation and roadmap routing
- implementation packages based on verified findings
- explicit uncertainty, review, and authorization controls

Require validation before use:

- market-leading
- proprietary accuracy advantage
- percentage improvement
- revenue or cost impact
- industry benchmark superiority
- evaluator agreement or reliability statistic
- guaranteed timeline

## 8. Synthetic and demonstration materials

- label fictional companies and synthetic data prominently
- do not use real logos or identities without permission
- do not imply completed external work
- keep `implementation_authorized: false` visible where applicable
- distinguish sample package scope from an actual priced offer

## 9. Partner and client branding

- client mark use requires permission
- client identity never changes evidence or publication rules
- co-branded reports retain Operator Intelligence methodology/version fields
- white-label use may not remove DecisionLedger, QC, publication, or limitation requirements

## 11. Prohibited brand behavior

- “guaranteed growth” or similar unsupported promise
- dramatic score graphics that conceal evidence coverage
- package promotion before finding/eligibility review
- AI-forward visual claims where AI is not implemented or approved
- client logos on synthetic or unapproved work
- outcome testimonials without source and permission
- presenting v1.0 readiness as independently certified unless such certification exists

## 12. Commercial v1.0 connection

These rules establish a credible, consistent market presentation that remains subordinate to the product’s evidence and governance architecture.
